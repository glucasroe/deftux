Day 85: iOS’s Update Scheduling

What it is: iOS will automatically download updates in the background, and then offer to install them when the device is charging that evening.

Why it’s good: Asking a user to update their device is always a bizarre ritual. We’re asking them to put it down and stop using it for a while, but the only time that they’ll see the message is when they’re already engaged. Seems counterproductive, doesn’t it?

This decision makes so much sense because for the vast majority of the user base, the device will be left unattended in the 5-hour window. This pop-up is effective because of how spare the copy is: direct but still warning of potential pitfalls (i.e. while connected to power)

Pushing programmatic and automated functions into a user’s downtime is a great way to make our products not feel like a chore to manage, or an errand to check off.

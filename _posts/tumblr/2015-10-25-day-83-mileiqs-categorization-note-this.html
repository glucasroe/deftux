Day 83: MileIQ’s Categorization

Note: This experience was recommended by my wife, Katelyn. Thanks Katelyn!

What it is: MileIQ is an application to help you keep track of your deductible mileage. It monitors your travel and gives you a breakdown of “Trips” which you categorize into “Business” and “Personal” by swiping. Longer swipes give you the ability to add metadata to the trips as you categorize them.

Why it’s good: This type of interface is now officially known as a “Tinder Swipe”—based off of the eponymous dating app. It’s a great application of the same idea in a different setting.

Reducing the amount of choices to a binary: “Business” or “Personal” is a wonderful statement of the app’s intent. Giving your user a simple, binary choice will always help them move through their data, especially when managing a long list of items. (Notice that she has over 100 drives to categorize)

The implementation of the longer swipe is ingenious. Some applications try to use longer swipes for different pieces of functionality. This is confusing because there isn’t usually a logical linkage between “Short swipe does x, long swipe does y.” There isn’t any map between the gesture and the outcome. This implementation spins it differently, swipe direction accomplishes the action, while longer swipes give you more detail. The mapping is very clear, a larger action in the same domain allows more functionality. Notice that it even maintains the state of the swiped out bar as it fades out the other screen elements.

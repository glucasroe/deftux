What it is: When moving multiple files, Google Docs animates them into a stack, and then shows the stack interacting with its supporting interface pieces.

Why it’s good: In the recent article Your UI isn’t a Disney Movie, Sophie Paxton brings up wonderful points about gratuitous animation in interfaces. She has a great point: animations that aren’t providing meaning are information noise, and end up wasting the user’s time.

In this case though, Google Drive uses small and smart animations to indicate the state of the UI. When working with a web interface, our users don’t usually understand the space of possibilities. Especially in web-based file systems, helping our users understand how they need to interact with the system is vital.The visual stacking happens quickly, and does a wonderful job of showing you where your files are going and what is happening to them.

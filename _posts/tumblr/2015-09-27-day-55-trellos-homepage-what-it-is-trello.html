What it is: Trello strips away all of their homepage content to a single pair of CTAs. When you’re logged in, it gives you the message “Hey, you’re logged in! Go to your boards.”

Why it’s good: Designing websites that allow logins are a fundamentally different task than more static sites. Every time you make a content decision, you have to ask “Is this right for both audiences?”

What’s so clever about this is subtle. You’ll notice that there’s a difference between the URLs because Trello.com automatically redirects you to your boards when you’re logged in. They know that you still might find your way to this homepage somehow, so they give you a simple and friendly way to get back. Thanks guys.

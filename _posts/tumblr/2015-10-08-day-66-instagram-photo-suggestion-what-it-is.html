What it is: When you open Instagram with recent photos in your library, a small window slides up that suggests you post photos that you’ve taken that day.

Why it’s good: For contextual suggestion, this is obviously solid. If the user has recently taken a photo, it stands to reason that they might be coming to Instagram to post it.

What makes this suggestion particularly good is in its flexibility. There are more apps for photo taking and editing than I can possible think of. There are more avenues to share photos than are reasonable. Allowing users multiple ways to arrive at the desired behavior (and in a case like this, prompting the desired behavior) makes the application feel flexible and robust.

What it is: The mindmapping tool XMind creates its data set in such a way that you can copy and paste data between it and a spreadsheet, like excel.

Why it is good: I know I’m running the risk of sounding like I’m just trying to sell this product, but hear me out. For about 2 years, I tried to find something that would just create a flow diagram from an excel file. I even tried building it on my own in Axure at one point. (with hilariously limited success)

Everything that I found was incredibly rigid. There were some things that showed promise, like yED or LucidChart, but in each case, I had to format my excel file in some arcane fashion. I did it in LucidChart at one point for a large navigation, and it took me about 3 hours. It sucked.

When I discovered that this product just let me work with my data in my own mental model, I was so excited I almost cried. I danced around for an entire afternoon. I copied it back and forth repeatedly just to watch it happen. Allow me to remind you, this is for a program that draws a chart.

When I read posts like this one “Delightful Interaction Design Needs to Die”, I’m reluctantly in agreement. True delight doesn’t come from a widget animating unexpectedly, that only provokes surprise. Delight comes from a something fitting into my needs, snapping into my mental model, and extending my ability to pursue my goals.

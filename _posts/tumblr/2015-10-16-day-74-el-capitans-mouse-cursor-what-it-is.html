What it is: When you wiggle your mouse quickly in OSX’s most recent update, the mouse pointer briefly becomes larger.

Why it’s good: Besides being incredibly helpful when you’re in a multiscreen environment, (Especially when you are hooked up to a projector) the reason that this particular design is so effective is that it mimics the current user behavior. When you can’t find your mouse pointer, you shake it around because eyes are better adapted to noticing motion.

The decision here works because the user didn’t have to learn and remember a new behavior. Instead, the designers at Apple paid attention to the current behavior and took another step towards bridging the gap between the software and the user.

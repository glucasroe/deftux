What it is: When entering a password on Amazon’s mobile app, it is defaulted to show the password in plain text below the obscured password.

Why it’s good: As an industry, we’ve pretty much settled that passwords are pretty terrible for usability, ease of use, and general experience. When we move the password field to a mobile screen, with its constraints on space and lack of tactile feedback, all of these problems are amplified.

These “View Password” toggles feel like a good solution, and they’re popping up with increasing regularity. This particular version of it is my favorite because it shows the plain text password underneath the obscured version. For users who are used to having their password obscured as they type, a plain text field gives them the wrong signal - it doesn’t look correct. Is it secure? Are they even on the password field right now?

This clarifies the relationship between the password and the plain text, and is a safer default, because it has the visual signifier that we’re used to.

What it is: When working in Confluence, there are a number of shortcuts available to you at any given time. Confluence indicates these shortcuts with subtle hints, like underlining a single letter or including it in parenthesis in a tooltip.

Why it’s good: Keyboard shortcuts are the consummate expert’s tool. For any application, there’s inevitably a cheat sheet of keyboard shortcuts somewhere that indicates everything you can do with them. I even have an index card on my desk with a list of shortcuts for Trello while I’m learning them.

Confluence isn’t a tool that most people live in, so expecting the user to memorize all of keyboard shortcuts isn’t realistic. This subtle indication doesn’t get in the way, but it does tell me what things I can do quickly. Incidentally, I’ve learned the keyboard shortcuts incredibly quickly because they’re always present.

What it is: When you tap command + q in Chrome, it doesn’t instantly close the app. Instead, it displays a banner which says “Hold ⌘Q to quit.”

Why it’s good: As a enthusiastic browser tab user, I am consistently hitting command + w to close tabs. Small challenge: next to the “w” on a keyboard is the “q,” and the system default for OSX is to quit the entire application when command + q is pressed. This usually means attempting to recover the ~30 or so sites that I have open at any given time. It was a consistent issue before Chrome introduced this feature.

This approach works because of its internal logic (i.e. you usually hit the wrong key when you’re in a hurry, so we can assume you won’t be holding it down) and the consistent message. Instead of just preventing the action, the system tells you why nothing happened when you pressed your key combination.

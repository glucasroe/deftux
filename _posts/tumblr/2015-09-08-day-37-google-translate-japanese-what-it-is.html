What it is: When translating Japanese text, Google Translate has you take a picture of it and then highlight the text with a finger.

Why it’s good: I have always communicated with a roman alphabet. I have no idea how to get a Japanese keyboard, or how to use it once I had it. How then do I enter in my Japanese characters for translation?

Enter Google Translate. I have literally no idea how I would have bridged this gap without this functionality. I don’t exactly know what purpose the highlighting holds, but as soon as you start working with it, it’s incredibly clear.

Side Note on Translation Quality: Yes, it’s not great. I used it to translate the instructions for a Japanese Water Stone I bought off of Amazon, and while broken and bizarre, I understood it well enough to have a santoku knife that I can now shave with. Brilliant!

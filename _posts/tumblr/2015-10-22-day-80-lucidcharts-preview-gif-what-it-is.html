What it is: When someone sends you a template from Lucidchart, it adds in a small gif that shows you all of the screens available in the template.

Why it’s good: This particular decision is so smart because it answers a very human question: “What is this?” In this particular case, the coworker who sent this to me hadn’t told me they were sending me something.

Instead of requiring some leap of faith, Lucidchart gave me a smart preview that let me know what I would find when I opened the template. Also, I’m not entirely sure how they worked this out, but the image that showed the screens wasn’t blocked by Outlook initially, so I saw the preview even in my inbox’s default state.

Predicting the questions that people ask isn’t easy, but it adds so much value to a product. “What is this?” is especially important when dealing with notification emails, or other messages that weren’t initiated by user action.

What it is: Neutrogena Naturals Face wash switches up how it handles the ingredients list, showing where the different elements come from.

Why it’s good: The average consumer has an odd relationship with the word “chemical.” We use the term as a stand in for a whole host of other bad terms; toxin, carcinogen, caustic, solvent, cleaner, the list goes on. Chemical names are just as scary, we don’t understand them so we assume it’s bad.

With the natural push in many products, manufacturers have an added difficulty of conveying what the ‘natural’ element of their product is. If I’m uninformed as a consumer, do I have any idea what “Cocamidopropyl Betaine” means? Definitely not, that sounds scary.

By listing where the ingredients come from, Neutrogena gives credibility to its claim. Instead of just seeing a lot of words that I don’t understand, I can see that it comes from a coconut. It’s a move that facilitates confidence, and helps me feel like they aren’t making baseless claims.

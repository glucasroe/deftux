What it is: When animating elements on a slide in Keynote, you can open a “Build Order” control that lets you preview the animations, reorder them, and edit them quickly.

Why it’s good: A constant design goal of mine is balancing granularity with mass editing tools. Allowing flexibility while clearly indicating what is possible is incredibly valuable.

In this case, Keynote gives you an immensely powerful and flexible animation structure without locking you into a single method of working with it. Animations are created on the element level, tweaking functions and setting the build order via a dropdown in the right information panel. It’s useful, but very specific to the individual elements and doesn’t give you a sense of the slide as a whole.

This interface gives you that high-level view, allowing you to focus specifically on the animation order and methods at a slide level. The “Preview” button is brilliantly implemented, showing you an accurate representation of slide state and also showing you when and how the animations are happening. Selecting the animations for the individual elements also selects the element on the stage, showing you which element you are engaging with.

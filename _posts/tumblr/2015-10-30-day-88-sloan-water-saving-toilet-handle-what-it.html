Day 88: Sloan Water Saving Toilet Handle

What it is: Sloan water saving handles are green, and have a label that indicate which direction you should push the handle to use the water saving function.

Why it’s good: This one feels a little out there, but bear with me. When things look the same, we don’t pay attention to them. They fade into the background and we use them automatically.

Usually, these water saving toilets use a circle cut in two halves. I have no idea what they mean, and I just guess at which of the two buttons to push. I’m wrong a lot.

In this case though, they use the interface I know. The combination of the color shift and the label immediately indicated that this should be used differently. The unifying element is the smart default though, when you pull the handle towards you starts a water saving flush. It’s a safe bet, and definitely the behavior that both the designer (and the store owner) want to encourage.

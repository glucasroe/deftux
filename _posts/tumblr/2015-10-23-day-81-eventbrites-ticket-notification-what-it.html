What it is: When you have an event coming up, Eventbrite sends you a notification. The notification has a QR code in it that serves as your ticket.

Why it’s good: When attending an event, you have a lot going on. Frequently, you are going to a place that you aren’t familiar with, and that brings a lot of complexity. Considering the complexity is usually managed via your phone (with Maps, Calendars, and messaging apps), being required to keep a ticket up on your phone is a liability.

This notification popped up just before the event, and as you can see, they actually put the barcode for my ticket in the notification. This inclusion means that if I was in the midst of navigating or communicating with others, I still have the ticket right there to quickly check in when I find the place.

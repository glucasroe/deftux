Day 17: 500px Terms & Privacy

What it is: 500px is a photo sharing and discovery site. Their terms and conditions are a 2 column page, the left column is all the legal-ese required to run a large site, and they then explain it on the right.

Why it’s good: EULA’s and legal text in general are terrible experiences. If each interpersonal relationship began with a 10 minute recitation of what you were agreeing to by entering into this relationship, being a hermit would be much more socially acceptable.

Some products decide to address this by shaming the user into reading the EULA that they’ve agreed to sight unseen. While it makes a product developer feel better, it doesn’t really encourage anyone to enter the situation with more knowledge. 500px takes a different route, it gets that you want to understand what you’re agreeing to, and then gives you the ability to bridge that gap.

The brilliant part of this design is in the juxtaposition. Instead of just writing a long document and then a simplified version, laying it side by side makes me feel as a user that this is an accurate translation. The large copy and short sentences make it easy to scan and understand. It gives me a quicker way to get the same information, which is brilliant.

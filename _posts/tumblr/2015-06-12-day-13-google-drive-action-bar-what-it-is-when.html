What it is: When selecting items in Google Drive, a small toolbar pops up that allows you to star the item, pin it (Which saves it to the device,) share it, or a host of other functions that can be popped up from the side menu.

Why it’s good: Google Drive takes a big risk in the layout of its various files. Because it uses a tile based approach, many of the features that users have come to expect aren’t as readily available in options on the tiles themselves.

Instead of trying to wedge a bunch of icons on the tile itself or taking you to a different screen (Where you wouldn’t have any mass editing options,) this toolbar accurately balances multiple use cases. While it is unfortunate that these icons don’t tell you what they do, the compromise of granular single file activities and mass editing is brilliant.
